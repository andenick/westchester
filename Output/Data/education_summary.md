# Westchester County Education Summary

Westchester County, New York, boasts a robust and diverse educational landscape, offering a wide range of options from pre-school to post-graduate studies. The county is home to 40 public school districts, 118 private schools, 12 colleges and universities, and 3 theological seminaries.

The educational facilities in Westchester County are recognized for their excellence, with students consistently performing well in Regents examinations and achieving SAT scores that surpass state and national averages. A significant proportion of high school students in the county pursue higher education, with some districts reporting nearly 100% college enrollment.

The public school districts vary in size and expenditure per pupil, all sharing a commitment to high standards. Many teachers in Westchester County hold master's degrees or higher, contributing to the quality of instruction. Notably, 28 of the top 100 school districts in New York State in 2018 were located in Westchester County. Some highly-rated public schools and districts include Scarsdale Senior High School, Horace Greeley High School, and Bronxville High School.

For those seeking higher education, Westchester County offers numerous institutions, including:
*   College of Westchester
*   Fordham University (West Harrison campus)
*   Iona University
*   Manhattanville University
*   Mercy University
*   New York Medical College
*   Pace University (Pleasantville and White Plains campuses)
*   Purchase College, State University of New York
*   Sarah Lawrence College
*   Westchester Community College

The county also provides vocational programs through local school districts and the Board of Cooperative Educational Services (BOCES). Additionally, resources like the Child Care Council of Westchester are available to help parents find quality child care and after-school programs.
