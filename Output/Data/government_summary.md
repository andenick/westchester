# Westchester County Government Summary

## Official Website

The official website for Westchester County Government is https://www.westchestergov.com/.

## Government Structure

Westchester County's government structure is comprised of legislative and executive branches, along with numerous departments, offices, commissions, and boards that deliver a wide range of services to its residents.

Key components of the Westchester County government include:
*   **Legislative Branch** The lawmaking body is the 17-member Board of Legislators. Each legislator is elected for a two-year term and represents a district of approximately 59,000 residents. This board is responsible for setting policies, determining the annual budget, approving appointments made by the County Executive, and establishing general government policies.
*   **Executive Branch** This branch is led by the County Executive, who is responsible for implementing the policies established by the Board of Legislators.
*   **Elected Officials** Besides the County Executive and the Board of Legislators, other elected officials include the County Clerk and the District Attorney.
*   **Departments and Agencies** The county government includes 25 specialized departments and approximately 25 additional offices, commissions, and boards. These entities provide essential services in areas such as health, public safety, social services, and employment.

The county seat for Westchester County is White Plains.
