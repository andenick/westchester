# Westchester County Economy Summary

## Key Economic Indicators:
*   **Population:** As of 2023, Westchester County had a population of 996,888.
*   **Median Household Income:** The median household income in Westchester County was $118,411 in 2023, showing a 3.28% increase from 2022.
*   **Median Property Value:** The median property value in the county was $638,400 in 2023, significantly higher than the national average.
*   **Job Growth:** From 2001 to 2022, total jobs in Westchester County increased by 21%. While this lagged behind the state and national averages during that period, job growth accelerated between 2021 and 2022, increasing by 5.3%.

## Major Industries and Employment:
The county's economy is driven by several key sectors:
*   **Healthcare and Social Assistance:** This is the most common employment sector, with 82,872 people employed in 2022-2023. The healthcare industry is projected to continue as a leading growth sector, generating significant economic impact and jobs.
*   **Educational Services:** This sector employed 64,504 people in 2022-2023.
*   **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services:** This sector employed 56,318 people in 2022-2023.
*   **Financial Activities:** This sector experienced substantial job gains of 103% between 2001 and 2022. White Plains, in particular, is a developing financial and business center.
*   **Leisure and Hospitality:** This sector saw a 33% increase in jobs from 2001 to 2022.
*   **Other Growing Sectors:** Clean energy, life sciences, and construction are also identified as industries with significant growth potential. Manufacturing, despite past job losses, is a prioritized industry with efforts to address labor shortages and skill gaps.

## Challenges and Disparities:
While Westchester County boasts a strong economy, disparities exist:
*   **Job Losses:** The Information and Manufacturing sectors experienced job declines of 38% and 41% respectively between 2001 and 2022.
*   **Unemployment and Income Disparities:** The overall poverty rate was low at 9% in 2018-22, but it was higher among African American (16%) and Hispanic (13%) populations. Unemployment rates were highest among African American workers (7.9%) and Hispanic workers (6.7%) in 2018-22, compared to white (5.3%) and Asian (4.8%) workers. Median incomes also show disparities, with Asian and white residents having higher median incomes than Hispanic and African American residents.
*   **Gender Wage Gap:** Females in Westchester earn, on average, 66 cents for every dollar earned by a male.

## Economic Development Initiatives:
Westchester County has ongoing economic development initiatives focused on increasing affordable housing and redeveloping downtown areas. The White Plains area continues to grow as a financial and business hub.
